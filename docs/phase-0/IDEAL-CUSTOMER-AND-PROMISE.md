# Ideal customer and product promise

## Opening customer

An Indian service business, agency, consultant, or small SaaS company with one legal entity, INR as the primary currency, one or two financial accounts, Stripe/Razorpay or direct bank transfers, an existing accountant/CA, and roughly 100–1,000 monthly movements.

## Required fit

- No inventory-heavy accounting.
- Founder can answer business-purpose questions.
- Accountant can review policy-sensitive cases.
- Current month-end process depends on spreadsheets, bank statements, messages, or manual categorization.
- Customer accepts review-first operation rather than autonomous posting.

## Reject or defer

Retail inventory, manufacturing, restaurant cash operations, construction project accounting, lending, marketplaces holding customer funds, crypto-heavy activity, multi-entity groups, complex foreign subsidiaries, and businesses seeking tax filing or payroll automation.

## Promise

Ledgerly imports and preserves transaction evidence, normalizes feeds, finds repeated patterns, asks only for missing context, reconciles related movements, and produces an explainable accountant-ready month-end package.

## Measurable outcome

For an in-scope service business, reduce founder transaction-review time to approximately 20 minutes per month while maintaining complete row accountability and high-confidence suggestion precision of at least 98% in the frozen evaluation corpus.

## Non-promises

Ledgerly does not replace a CA, provide final tax advice, guarantee deductibility, file returns, silently edit closed periods, fabricate reconciliation entries, or auto-post uncertain journals.

## Buyer and users

- Economic buyer: founder or finance owner.
- Primary operator: founder reviewer or bookkeeper.
- Policy reviewer: accountant/CA.
- Audit observer: read-only reviewer where required.
